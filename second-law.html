Force Equals Mass Times Acceleration

Newton's Second Law states: "The acceleration of an object as produced by a net force is directly proportional to the magnitude of the net force, in the same direction as the net force, and inversely proportional to the mass of the object."

This is often written as the famous equation: F = ma

Where:

  • F = force (in Newtons, N)
  • m = mass (in kilograms, kg)
  • a = acceleration (in meters per second squared, m/s²)

Examples from Daily Life:

  • It takes more force to push a heavy box than a light one
  • A rocket needs tremendous force to accelerate because of its large mass
  • Braking distance increases with speed because deceleration requires more force
