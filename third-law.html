Action and Reaction

Newton's Third Law states: "For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction."

This means that when one object exerts a force on a second object, the second object exerts a force of equal magnitude in the opposite direction on the first object.

Key Points:

  • Forces always occur in pairs
  • Action and reaction forces act on different objects
  • The forces are equal in magnitude but opposite in direction

Examples from Daily Life:

  • Walking: You push the ground backward, the ground pushes you forward
  • Rocket propulsion: Gases push down, rocket goes up
  • Swimming: You push water backward, water pushes you forward

Action-Reaction Pairs

Action: Foot pushes backward on ground
⇅
Reaction: Ground pushes forward on foot
Action: Hand pushes on wall
⇅
Reaction: Wall pushes on hand
Action: Book pushes down on table
⇅
Reaction: Table pushes up on book
