The Law of Inertia

Newton's First Law states: "An object at rest stays at rest and an object in motion stays in motion with the same speed and in the same direction unless acted upon by an unbalanced force."

This law is often called the Law of Inertia. Inertia is the tendency of an object to resist changes in its state of motion.

Examples from Daily Life:

  • When a car stops suddenly, passengers lurch forward
  • A tablecloth can be pulled out from under dishes
  • It's harder to push a full shopping cart than an empty one
